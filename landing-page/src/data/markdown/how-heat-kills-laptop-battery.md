# How Heat Kills Your Laptop Battery Faster Than Overcharging

We talk a lot about the 80% charging rule, but there is a silent killer that destroys batteries even faster: **Heat**.

## The Chemistry of Thermal Degradation

Lithium-ion batteries operate best at room temperature (around 20°C to 25°C). When you push your laptop by rendering videos, compiling code, or playing games, the internal temperature can spike above 45°C.

At these temperatures, the chemical reactions inside the battery cell become unstable. The electrolyte begins to break down, permanently reducing the battery's ability to hold a charge.

## The Deadly Combo: Heat + 100% Charge

If you leave your laptop plugged in at 100% while running heavy workloads, you are combining max voltage stress with max thermal stress. This is the fastest way to cause a battery to physically swell.

Use an app like CasYuk to cap your charge at 80% when doing heavy work at a desk. By removing the voltage stress, the battery can handle the thermal stress much better.
