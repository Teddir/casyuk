# MacBook Battery Limiter: Why You Need to Stop Charging at 80%

If you use your MacBook at a desk most of the day, leaving it plugged in at 100% is slowly destroying its lithium-ion battery. 

## The 80% Rule

Lithium-ion cells experience the most chemical stress when they are near empty (below 20%) or completely full (above 80%). When a battery sits at 100% capacity while receiving constant power, the internal voltage stress accelerates degradation, leading to lost capacity and even physical swelling.

## Why Built-in Features Aren't Enough

macOS has "Optimized Battery Charging", but it relies on highly predictable schedules. If your routine varies even slightly, macOS defaults to pushing your battery straight to 100%.

## The Solution

Using a **MacBook battery limiter** like CasYuk forces the system's SMC (System Management Controller) to physically stop accepting charge at 80%. This mimics the battery preservation systems found in electric vehicles like Teslas. By keeping your battery in the "golden zone" (20% - 80%), you can significantly extend its cycle life.
