# Is It Bad to Leave Your MacBook Plugged In All the Time?

The short answer: **Yes, it is.**

## The 100% Myth

Many people believe that modern laptops have "smart" bypass circuits that stop charging when the battery is full. While it's true that laptops stop drawing power to the battery once it hits 100%, the battery itself is still sitting at its maximum voltage capacity.

Imagine eating until you are 100% full, and then being forced to sit at a table smelling food for 24 hours. Your stomach is constantly stretched to its absolute limit. That's what your battery feels like.

## How to Fix It

If you use your laptop as a desktop replacement, you **must** use a battery limiter. Software like CasYuk forces the hardware to stop charging at 80%, allowing the battery to relax while the laptop runs entirely off wall power.
